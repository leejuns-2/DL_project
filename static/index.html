Foundation Model application

MiniLM turns energy report paragraphs into traceable market-context signals.

This project applies a selected foundation model to the energy-report domain. MiniLM-L6-v2 embeds PDF paragraphs, a few-shot head adapts those vectors into energy-theme signals, and the output is linked to historical context around the report date.

01 - Foundation Model Method
Foundation model MiniLM-L6-v2 representation

A frozen sentence embedding model converts each PDF paragraph into a dense semantic vector.

Evidence retrieval TF-IDF assisted filtering

TF-IDF narrows long PDFs to candidate evidence paragraphs before MiniLM-based scoring is applied.

Few-shot adaptation Task-specific logistic head

Small human examples train a lightweight head on top of frozen embeddings instead of full fine-tuning.

Historical context Report-date market link

Signals are joined to past news tone and return windows for context, not prediction.

Method notes

In this project, the foundation model is used as a representation engine, not as a predictor: it converts unstructured report paragraphs into semantic vectors, and the few-shot classifier translates those vectors into auditable energy-theme signals.

Why MiniLM? It is lightweight, sentence-level, and suitable for paragraph similarity and theme classification.
Why not full fine-tuning? The project has limited labeled examples, so a frozen FM plus a few-shot head is more realistic and auditable.
Why TF-IDF? It is a retrieval aid that reduces noise before the foundation-model representation step.
Why market data? It supplies historical context around report dates, not causal proof or future prediction.

Research findings

  1. Energy reports contain paragraphs that can be converted into theme-level signals.
  2. Frozen MiniLM embeddings are useful for representing PDF evidence paragraphs.
  3. A small few-shot logistic head improves theme alignment compared with zero-shot similarity.
  4. Historical market windows are useful as context, but should not be interpreted as causal evidence or future prediction.
  5. Main failure cases occur in generic ESG paragraphs, ambiguous grid-transition language, and date-metadata errors.
